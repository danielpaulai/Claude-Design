# Brand In A Box — Intake (filled example: Marigold & Oak)

This is the filled intake for a Warm Editorial brand.

Use it as a reference if your brand sits in the wellness, lifestyle, or women-led space.

---

## 1 · The brand

**1. Business name.**
Answer: Marigold & Oak.

**2. One-line description.**
Answer: I help women-led wellness brands grow without losing their soul.

**3. Who you serve, in one sentence.**
Answer: Female founders running $50K to $500K wellness, yoga, retreat, or holistic health businesses where the brand is an extension of the founder's values.

---

## 2 · The transformation

**4. The pain you solve.**
Answer: My brand looks like a Pinterest mood board, not a business. I keep changing it.

**5. The promise.**
Answer: A coherent brand and content engine in 6 weeks. One that grows without your daily input.

---

## 3 · How it should look and sound

**6. Pick one aesthetic family.**
Answer: Warm Editorial.

**7. Three writing samples.**

Answer (paste below):

```
There is a moment in every wellness founder's first year where she realises her brand is not a brand. It is a Pinterest board.

I have stood in that moment myself.

My client Maya runs a small retreat centre in Oregon. Last year she tried five different logos. Her revenue stayed flat. We rewrote her positioning in two days, and her bookings doubled in a quarter. The logo never came up again.

You can run a soulful business and still have a real brand. The two are not enemies. They never were.
```
