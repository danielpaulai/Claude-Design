# Marigold & Oak — Positioning

Generated by brand-in-a-box from INTAKE Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4, Q5.

## One-line positioning

I help women-led wellness brands grow without losing their soul.

## Category

Brand strategy and content systems for women-led wellness, lifestyle, and holistic businesses.

Inferred anti-positioning: not a logo designer. Not a marketing agency. Not a "personal branding coach."

Marigold & Oak sits at the intersection. The brand strategist who actually ships the system, in the founder's voice, without flattening the soul of the work.

## Who we serve

Female founders running $50K to $500K wellness, yoga, retreat, or holistic health businesses where the brand is an extension of the founder's values.

See [icp.md](icp.md).

## The pain we solve

My brand looks like a Pinterest mood board, not a business. I keep changing it.

The founder has taste. She has spent hours on Pinterest, on Instagram, on competitors' websites. She has a colour palette in three apps. She has changed her logo four times. She still cannot describe her brand in one sentence.

## The promise

A coherent brand and content engine in 6 weeks. One that grows without your daily input.

## Why us instead of the alternatives (inferred)

| Alternative | Why founders choose us instead |
|---|---|
| Logo designer | A logo without a system is decoration. We give the system. |
| Marketing agency | Agencies optimise for vanity metrics. We optimise for booking calls. |
| "Brand coach" on Instagram | Coaches give you frameworks. We ship the asset. |
| Doing it themselves | Six weeks of structured work beats two years of solo iteration. |

## The shift we are selling

From: a brand that lives across five Pinterest boards, three Notion docs, and a head full of "I'll know it when I see it."

To: a brand that lives in one folder. Voice, colours, type, anti-slop rules. Drop it into any tool, any AI, any team member. The brand stays.

## Enemies (inferred from the pain in Q4)

- The Pinterest-board-as-brand trap.
- "Boss babe" wellness branding that flattens every founder into the same loud aesthetic.
- Logo-first thinking. The logo is the last thing, not the first.
- Manifesting language used to dodge real strategic decisions.

## Tone of the brand

Warm. Specific. Story-led. Direct without being aggressive.
