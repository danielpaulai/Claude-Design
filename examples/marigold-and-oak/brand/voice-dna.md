# Marigold & Oak — Voice DNA

Generated by brand-in-a-box from your three writing samples in INTAKE Q7.

Each rule below references its source.

## Hard rules

- **Story-led openings.** Extracted from your samples. Two of three open with "There is a moment..." or a client story. Lead with story, land with point.
- **Sentence rhythm: flowing, not staccato.** Extracted from your samples. Average 18 words per sentence. Sentences breathe. Not Taki Moore one-per-paragraph.
- **No em dashes.** Extracted from your samples. Zero across 3 samples. Use periods and commas.
- **Sentence case.** Extracted from your samples.
- **No emojis in professional copy.** Default for Warm Editorial.

## Pronouns and POV

Extracted from your samples. Counted: 4 first-person, 3 second-person, 3 third-person across 3 samples.

- **"I" + "you" + "she/he"** mix. You speak as yourself, but you also tell stories about clients in third person.
- "She" is allowed when narrating client examples ("My client Maya runs a retreat centre...").
- Never "we" except in positioning copy.

## Banned phrases

Inferred from your samples and 2026 AI tells. Your samples avoid these. Treat as hard bans.

`unlock` · `leverage` · `growth hack` · `10x` · `scale fast` · `crush it` · `boss babe` · `manifest` (used loosely) · `boss up` · `level up`

The wellness category is full of these. Avoid them harder than other categories would.

## Hook mechanisms (extracted from your samples)

Your samples use three patterns. Use them in this rough ratio:

1. **Universal-truth opener.** "There is a moment in every wellness founder's first year..." (sample 1). Names a feeling the reader has had.
2. **Client story.** "My client Maya runs a small retreat centre in Oregon..." (sample 3). Ground abstract claims in a specific person.
3. **Reframe / contrarian gentle.** "You can run a soulful business and still have a real brand." (sample 4). Soft contrarian, not aggressive.

## Rhythm

Sentences average 18 words. Let them breathe.

Use longer paragraphs (2-3 sentences each) for storytelling. Single sentences only for landings.

Sensory details are allowed and welcomed. "Last year she tried five different logos" beats "She struggled with branding."

Numbers stay specific but soft: "two days" not "48 hours," "in a quarter" not "in 90 days."

## CTA pattern

Soft. Specific. Conversational.

> If you have stood in that moment, you are not alone. Comment STORY and I will send you the framework we used with Maya.

Always followed by a P.S. that softens or adds context.

## On-brand example

> There is a moment in every wellness founder's first year where she realises her brand is not a brand. It is a Pinterest board.
>
> I have stood in that moment myself.

## Off-brand example (do not ship)

> Ready to manifest your dream brand and 10x your wellness empire? Unlock the secret sauce ✨ Boss babes only 💃 #wellness #manifesting

That sentence violates: banned phrase (manifest), banned phrase (10x), banned phrase (unlock), banned phrase (secret sauce), banned phrase (boss babes), emojis, hashtags, register-mismatch (overcooked / aggressive).
