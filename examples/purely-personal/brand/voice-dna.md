# Purely Personal — Voice DNA

Generated by brand-in-a-box from your three writing samples in INTAKE Q7.

Each rule below references its source.

## Hard rules

- **One sentence per paragraph.** Extracted from your samples. Average sentence length is 9 words. White space is part of the layout.
- **Plain English.** Extracted from your samples. No jargon, no buzzwords, no corporate-speak.
- **No em dashes.** Extracted from your samples. Zero em dashes in 3 samples. Default for 2026.
- **No hashtags on LinkedIn.** Default for 2026 personal-brand posts.
- **No emojis in professional copy.** Default for Editorial Minimal family.
- **Sentence case.** Extracted from your samples. Title case only for proper nouns and product names.

## Pronouns and POV

Extracted from your samples. Counted: 2 first-person, 4 second-person across 3 samples. Second-person dominant — you talk *to* the reader, not *about* yourself.

- **"I" + "you"** always. You speak as yourself, directly to a founder.
- Never "we" except in positioning copy (about pages, company-voice statements).
- Never third-person ("Founders who use Purely Personal...").

## Banned phrases

Inferred from your samples plus 2026 AI tells. Your samples notably avoid these. Treat as hard bans.

`growth hack` · `10x` · `guru` · `unlock` · `leverage` · `hustle` · `synergy` · `disrupt` · `game-changer` · `secret sauce` · `follow for more`

## Hook mechanisms (extracted from your samples)

Your samples use three patterns. Use them in this rough ratio:

1. **Contrarian observation.** "You spend 15 hours a week on LinkedIn." (sample 1)
2. **Transformation / I-built.** "I built an entire marketing team using Claude." (sample 2)
3. **Category reframe.** "In 2025 you needed a marketing team. In 2026 you don't." (sample 3)

## Rhythm

Sentences average 9 words. Keep them short.

Each sentence on its own line in posts (Taki Moore style).

Open with a hook in 8 to 12 words.

Land outcomes with numbers and timeframes: "15 hours → 2 hours", "12 → 2", "90 minutes."

## CTA pattern

Soft, specific, in-line with the post. Not a marketing ask.

> Comment "TEAM" and I'll send you the full skill stack via DM.
> Comment "AI" and I'll send you the workshop details.

Always followed by a P.S. that asks a question or offers a bonus.

## On-brand example

> You spend 15 hours a week on LinkedIn.
> But you're still not getting results.
> I got it down to 2 hours using Claude and Skills.

## Off-brand example (do not ship)

> Unlock next-level LinkedIn ROI with our 10x growth-hacking AI stack 🚀

That sentence violates: banned phrase (unlock), banned phrase (10x), banned phrase (growth hack), emoji, hashtag-adjacent stack-language.
