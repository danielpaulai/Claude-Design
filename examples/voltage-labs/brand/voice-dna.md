# Voltage Labs — Voice DNA

Generated by brand-in-a-box from your three writing samples in INTAKE Q7.

Each rule below references its source.

## Hard rules

- **Declarative, not persuasive.** Extracted from your samples. State the truth, do not argue for it. "AI does not replace your team. It changes which questions matter." is a statement, not a pitch.
- **Specific numbers always.** Extracted from your samples. $50M company. 3 hours to 35 minutes. 18 months. 6 weeks. Numbers are the brand's currency.
- **Sentence rhythm: short and load-bearing.** Extracted from your samples. Average 11 words. Each sentence does work.
- **No em dashes.** Extracted from your samples. Zero across 3 samples.
- **Sentence case.** Extracted from your samples.
- **No emojis in professional copy.** Default for Cinematic Dark. Especially for B2B technical audiences.

## Pronouns and POV

Extracted from your samples. Counted: 3 first-person plural ("we"), 1 second-person ("your"), neutral collective voice.

- **"We"** for company actions. "We deployed a forecasting agent..."
- **"You" / "your"** for the reader's situation. "It changes which questions matter [for your team]."
- Avoid first-person singular. The brand is the company, not the founder. (Different from Editorial Minimal personal brands.)

## Banned phrases

Inferred from your samples and 2026 AI tells. Your samples avoid these. Treat as hard bans.

`game-changing` · `revolutionary` · `cutting-edge` · `next-generation` · `synergy` · `seamless` · `frictionless` · `disrupt` · `unlock` · `leverage` · `holistic`

The B2B AI category is full of these. Avoid them harder than other categories would.

## Hook mechanisms (extracted from your samples)

Your samples use three patterns. Use them in this rough ratio:

1. **Reframe with authority.** "AI does not replace your team. It changes which questions matter." (sample 1). Two clauses, one negates the false premise, the second lands the truth.
2. **Specific case study.** "We deployed a forecasting agent for a $50M logistics company last quarter..." (sample 2). Real numbers, real outcome.
3. **Time-stakes warning.** "The mid-market has 18 months." (sample 3). Specific countdown, no hedging.

## Rhythm

Sentences average 11 words. Tight.

Use periods aggressively. "Same accuracy. Same call." beats "with the same accuracy and the same call."

Land outcomes in measurable units: hours, weeks, months, dollars, percent.

## CTA pattern

Direct and operational. No "click here," no "learn more."

> See if your data is ready. 20-minute fit call.

> Read the technical brief. PDF, 12 pages.

> Request a 6-week pilot. We do not pitch agencies.
