Generated by brand-in-a-box from INTAKE Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4, Q5.

## One-line positioning

I deploy production AI agents inside mid-market companies in 6 weeks instead of 6 months.

## Category

Production AI deployment for B2B mid-market.

Inferred anti-positioning: not a strategy consultancy. Not a generative AI seat-license. Not an LLM API wrapper.

Voltage Labs ships agents that run, in production, against the customer's data, against the customer's KPIs. The deck is the byproduct, not the deliverable.

## Who we serve

Operations and finance leaders at $20M to $200M B2B companies who have the data but not the in-house AI team.

See [icp.md](icp.md).

## The pain we solve

Our consultants gave us a 90-page strategy deck. Six months later we have nothing in production.

The customer has paid a Big-4 consultancy for an AI roadmap. They have a deck, an executive summary, a slide on "agentic workflows," and zero shipped agents. The board is asking what changed.

## The promise

Three production AI agents shipped in 6 weeks. Measured in hours saved per week, not slides delivered.

## The shift we are selling

From: a 90-page strategy deck and a 12-month roadmap that goes nowhere.

To: three agents running in production by week six. Measurable hours saved per week. A team that knows how to ship the next ten.

## Enemies (inferred from the pain in Q4)

- Strategy decks pretending to be implementation.
- The consulting industry billing on hours instead of outcomes.
- "AI transformation" as a vague marketing wrapper around no actual deployment.
- Vendor seat licenses sold as solutions.

## Tone of the brand

Declarative. Numbers-first. Technical without being inaccessible. No hype, no hedge.
