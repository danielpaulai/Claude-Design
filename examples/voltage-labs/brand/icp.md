# Voltage Labs — Ideal Client Profile

Generated by brand-in-a-box from INTAKE Q3, Q4, Q5.

## Who we serve

Operations and finance leaders at $20M to $200M B2B companies who have the data but not the in-house AI team.

More specifically:

- Director / VP / Chief Operating Officer / Chief Financial Officer
- Companies with $20M to $200M revenue, B2B, 50-1500 employees
- Industries: logistics, manufacturing, B2B SaaS, professional services, financial services
- Mature data infrastructure (Snowflake, BigQuery, or equivalent — not still on Excel)
- Has tried at least one consulting engagement on AI in the last 18 months and has nothing in production

## Where they are today (extracted from your pain in Q4)

Their consultants gave them a 90-page strategy deck. Six months later they have nothing in production.

In practice:

- Board has asked "where are we on AI?" three quarters in a row
- ChatGPT Enterprise seats deployed, no measurable outcome
- One internal AI/ML hire considered, none made (or made and stalled)
- 2-3 vendor pilots started, none deployed
- Deck has the word "agentic" in it, but no agents exist

## Their deeper pains

- "Our board keeps asking and I keep showing the same slide."
- "We paid the consultancy $400K and got a deck."
- "Every vendor pitch sounds the same. None of them ship."
- "I do not know which problems are AI-shaped and which are not."
- "We have the data. We have the budget. We do not have the people who can build the thing."

## The transformation they want (extracted from your promise in Q5)

Three production AI agents shipped in 6 weeks. Measured in hours saved per week, not slides delivered.

This means:

- Agents running in production against the company's actual data
- Hours saved per week, measured
- A team trained to ship the next ten agents without external help
- Board update with screenshots of running systems, not slide titles

## What they will pay for

- Fixed-fee 6-week pilots ($75K-$200K range)
- Production deployment, not strategy decks
- Enablement of the in-house team during the pilot
- A clear measurement framework (hours saved, error rate, throughput)

## What they will not buy

- Time-and-materials consulting
- Strategy without implementation
- "AI seat licenses" without integration
- Anything that pitches "the future of work"

## Geography and channel

Primarily US, UK, EU. Mid-market HQs.

Discover via referrals from previous customers, podcast appearances on B2B operations shows, conference keynotes at logistics / manufacturing / fintech events.

Convert via 20-minute "fit call" → 6-week pilot proposal → signed SOW.

## Disqualifiers

- Pre-revenue or sub-$20M companies (we are not a startup tooling vendor)
- Enterprise > $200M with internal data science teams (they should build it themselves)
- Companies still on spreadsheets (no data foundation)
- Buyers who want to be "AI thought leaders" rather than ship working systems
